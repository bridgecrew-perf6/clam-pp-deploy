A Foodstand Clam

A web game based on running a food stand on Maple Island. A fun project with the purpose of learning to code in vanilla Javascript. Built from scratch.

LIVE DEMO SOURCE CODE
Tech Stack
  • Built with Javascript, HTML, and CSS
  • Version control and deployment with Git
Technical Achievments
  • Created gameloop with a stabilized frame rate of 100fps on all computers/browsers. Did this by implementing a gameloop which ingests the current timestamp, and checks the difference between the last timestamp. If the change in time is >= 10 milliseconds, the next frame is updated. As a new frame is loaded 100 times a second, the game framerate is cemented at 100 frames per second regardless of the user's browser speed.

  • Implemented a simple gamestate design pattern to manage the different phases of the game. At any given time, one gamestate is active (like a Pause gamestate, Menu gamestate, etc.), and going to other gamestates changes elements like the background image, active functions, etc.

A Geeky Beginning

In my youth, there were a few landmark moments that predisposed me to a career in technology. and predisposed me to being a nerdy guy in general

First was around the age of 10, when my parents brought home a Windows Vista PC.

It was heavy, beige, and was strictly for "homework only". It wasn't event connected to the internet.

But that didn't stop me from spending hours playing with Space Pinball, Minesweeper, and making custom Starcraft maps.

A typical Windows PC in the 2000's, just like the one my parents picked up in 2006

The next few landmark moments were during high school. I got my own computer with wifi connection, and I could not get enough of it Just ask my parents .

Eventually it led me into my first internet-acquired skill: PC building. When I was 15, with the power of Google, Youtube, and Reddit, I ordered parts and built my first custom PC.

I showed myself that, with just a computer, the internet, and some good 'ol dedication, I could learn almost anything.

Youtube was, and still is, a treasure trove of knowledge. This is what it looked like back when I was 15-- man, I'm getting old.


Through my later high school years, continued my self-studies in technology and computing. I helped people with their computer problems, learned to build websites Solely with Wordpress. Thinking back on those websites makes me cringe-- they were bad., and even dabbled in programming. Though nothing ever came of it; just learned some python syntax and made some ultra-basic projects


However, by the time graduation rolled around and it was time to choose my next steps, I elected to study business.




"Wait, what? You went to a business school?"

Yep.

My mentor I had in my youth (and still have to this day) was an entrepreneur, and many of my role models were business-people, so it felt very natural for me to go to business school.

And although I was a shy student, I figured it'd help me open up more, and gain social confidence. It did. But, not going to lie, it was really difficult being in business school as an introvert / socially anxious person. However, I'm glad I did it, and there's no question it made me more socially capable,

I had my eyes set on the Business Technology Management major at UBC's prestigious Sauder business school. Here was my perfect match, I thought. I can study business and technology concurrently. Perfect.

UBC Sauder School of Business. The dream school of many money-hungry BC school kids

But I didn't make the grades to get in. I was dissapointed to say the least.

Instead, I went to Capilano University for my first year. I studied like mad. I made the grades.

The next year, I successfully transferred into UBC Sauder, and entered the Business Technology Management concentration; my dream school, and dream program.

First foray into the industry

I was then lucky enough to get into the co-op program.
